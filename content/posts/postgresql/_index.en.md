PostgreSQL is not just an open source database.<br>
It is the result of nearly four decades of academic and industrial evolution.<br>

Born in 1986 at the University of Berkeley as an evolution of Ingres, the original POSTGRES project introduced concepts that were ahead of its time: extensibility, custom data types, rules and an advanced relational model.<br>

In 1996 SQL support was added and the name became PostgreSQL.<br>
The world, however, kept calling it simply “Postgres”.<br>
And that’s perfectly fine.<br>

In this section I explore PostgreSQL from an architectural and operational perspective: design, performance, security and technical decisions applicable to real-world environments.<br>

Because choosing PostgreSQL is not just choosing an open source database.<br>
It is choosing an engine designed to be extended, analyzed and truly understood.
