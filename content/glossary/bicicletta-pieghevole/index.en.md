A **Folding Bike** is a bicycle designed to fold into compact dimensions (typically 60×55×25 cm) in seconds, becoming transportable as luggage. The Brompton is the best-known model, with a folding mechanism that takes 10-20 seconds.

## How it works

A system of hinges and quick releases allows folding the frame, handlebars, and pedals into a compact package. Once folded, it goes into the office under the desk, onto the metro, or into a car trunk. In the electric version, it combines the advantages of pedal assist with total portability.

## What it's for

It completely eliminates the parking problem — which in Rome can cost €35 per day and an hour and a half of searching. There is no theft risk because the bike is always with you. And on heavy rain days, it folds up and goes on the metro without issues.

## Why it matters

The folding bike is the "superpower" that makes bike commuting practical even for those without dedicated parking, those who need public transport for part of the journey, or those working in offices without bike racks. It is the last-mile solution that cars cannot offer.
